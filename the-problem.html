The Problem

Duplicate data entry is costing your lab more than you think

When staff re-key the same patient, order, and result data across LIMS, instruments, and billing systems, every handoff adds time, errors, and revenue risk.

Staff time lost to re-entry

Clinical and administrative staff routinely enter the same information into multiple platforms — especially when systems do not share data natively. The 2024 CAQH Index estimates that fully automated administrative workflows save an average of 70 minutes per patient visit compared to manual processes.

$20 billion: Annual savings opportunity if the U.S. healthcare industry shifted routine admin tasks from manual to electronic workflows (2024 CAQH Index, covering $90B in tracked transaction spending).

Errors compound at every handoff

Manual transcription is a leading source of claim issues. Industry analyses consistently cite coding errors, eligibility gaps, and duplicate submissions among top denial drivers. XiFin's 2025 Payor Denial Impact Report (20M+ claims, 2024 dates of service) found that duplicate denials represented nearly 20% of denials in clinical and molecular lab segments — even as overall denial rates improved year over year.

Denials and rework drain revenue

Kodiak Solutions reported that the initial denial rate rose to 11.81% in 2024 across 2,100+ hospitals and 300,000 physicians on its platform. Premier, Inc. data cited by industry summaries show the administrative cost per denied claim increased to $57.23 in 2023, up from $43.84 in 2022. AHIMA Journal estimates the cost to rework a single denied claim ranges from $25 to $181.

Why labs feel this acutely

Diagnostic labs sit at the intersection of clinical data, payer rules, and high-volume orders. A single typo in a patient identifier or CPT mapping can delay reimbursement for weeks. When billing teams receive data that was manually copied from another system, they inherit errors they did not create — and spend hours fixing them.
