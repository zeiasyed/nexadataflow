Industry Impact

How much waste labs inherit from a fragmented system

Healthcare's administrative complexity is not a lab-specific failure — but clinical laboratories pay a disproportionate price when data cannot move cleanly between systems.

~30%Of U.S. healthcare spending attributed to administrative costs (California Health Care Foundation overview)
$21BEstimated administrative waste in California's healthcare system alone (2020 research, CHCF)
19%Of in-network claims denied by ACA marketplace insurers in 2023 (KFF analysis)

Incompatible systems create systemic waste

The California Health Care Foundation notes that hospitals, physicians, and payers often use different systems that cannot easily share information. The result: providers waste time tracking down records, patients repeat histories, and organizations absorb cost without adding clinical value.

Manual processes dominate revenue cycle work

CAQH tracks $90 billion in annual spending on routine administrative transactions in medical and dental industries. Despite progress, manual tasks in eligibility verification, prior authorization, and claims submission remain a major cost driver — and a major source of delay for labs waiting on clean data before they can bill.

Denial trends affect every segment

While clinical outreach lab denial rates improved to 6.5% in 2024 (XiFin), broader provider denial rates continue to climb. Kodiak Solutions pegged the 2024 initial denial rate at 11.81%. For labs, even a low denial rate multiplied across high test volume translates to significant revenue at risk.

What full integration changes

Organizations that connect EHR, LIMS, and billing platforms report fewer charge capture errors, faster claims, and less staff time reconciling spreadsheets. NexaDataFlow brings that integration mindset to clinical labs — with people on the ground to make it stick.
